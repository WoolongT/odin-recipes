Yum yum Deviled Eggs Can't beat a good deviled egg from Yum Yum! I like to pair a few with a nice Firecap soup on those rainy nights.
  • 12 Eggs
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • Two tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon parsley
  • One gren onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon dill weed
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika, plus more for dusting
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
  1. Place the eggs in a pot of cold water. Make sure not to overcrowd them
  2. Heat over medium heat until the water starts to boil. Shut off the heat and cover the pot with a secure lid. Allow the eggs to sit for ten minutes in the water.
  3. Remove the eggs and allow them to cool before peeling.
  4. Slice the peeled eggs in half. Remove the yolks and arrange the whites on a large baking sheet or plate.
  5. Add the yolks and all the other ingredients to a large plastic storage bag and knead together until well mixed. (Alternately you can mix it all in a bowl and spoon the filling into the whites.)
  6. Cut off a small corner of the bag and pipe the yolk mixture into the whites. Sprinkle with paprika. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired. Refrigerate leftovers.
